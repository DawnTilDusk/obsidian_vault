# Persuasive Speech Part 1: Speech Process & Outline

## I. Topic Choice & Purpose (5 pts)

- **Topic**: Young People Should Challenge Traditional Career Expectations
- **General Purpose**: to persuade
- **Specific Purpose**: convince my audience to challenge traditional career stereotypes, explore interest-aligned career paths, and communicate their career goals with family sincerely
- **Elevator Speech (2–3 sentences)**: We will spend 90,000 hours at work—nearly one third of our lives. Challenging traditional career expectations isn’t reckless; it helps us avoid burnout, seize emerging opportunities, and build a fulfilling life that aligns with our desires, not just others’ approval.

---

## II. Outline (5 pts) - Follow Monroe’s Motivated Sequence

1. **Hook (Attention Getter)**: Close your eyes for a second and imagine that...

2. **Relevancy (Why it matters to audience)**: As classmates, we are about to enter the job market, and our career choices will define 1/3 of our lives. Blindly following traditional career norms risks spending those years on empty “stability” instead of fulfilling work—something none of us want.

3. **Thesis Statement**: Young people must courageously challenge traditional career expectations.

4. **Credibility**: I referenced data from China Youth Daily’s 2024 report and the World Economic Forum, shared real-life stories (my uncle, classmate Alex, my own experience), and cited examples of successful non-traditional careers (Unitree Robotics founder, _Black Myth: Wukong_ digital artists).

5. **Preview of Main Points**: Today, I’ll explain the two big risks of following traditional career expectations, share 3 practical steps to challenge those norms wisely, and visualize the positive future this choice brings.

6. **Core Content**:
    - **Problem/Need**: 1. Blindly following traditional careers leads to career burnout (e.g., my uncle’s unfulfilling 10-year bank teller job); 2. It causes us to miss emerging opportunities (e.g., AI trainers, metaverse designers) while traditional jobs shrink (35% drop in Chinese bank tellers in 5 years); 3. It erodes independent decision-making skills (e.g., Alex, forced into finance by parents).
    - **Solution**: 1. Follow what makes you “lose track of time” (not parents’ “you’re good at this”)—like Unitree Robotics founder Wangxingxing; 2. Research to build a viable plan (e.g., take a 3-month video editing course for short-video production); 3. Talk to parents as partners (share your passion and plans, like I did with my parents about engineering).
    - **Visualization**: Imagine waking up excited on Mondays, working on projects you care about (e.g., making short videos, building eco-friendly AI)—tired but proud, and telling your parents honestly, “I’m fulfilling.” As a group, we’ll fill 65% of 2030’s new high-demand jobs (per World Economic Forum) with our passion.
    - **Call to Action**: This week, spend 30 minutes writing what you love, research one career tied to it, and talk to your parents about it sincerely.

7. **Conclusion**:
    - **Review of Main Points**: Traditional career norms bring burnout and missed opportunities; challenging them with interest-alignment, research, and family communication leads to fulfillment; we’ll shape 2030’s job market.
    - **Restate Thesis**: Young people must courageously challenge traditional career expectations to live fulfilling, authentic lives.
    - **Final Line**: We do not follow the future—we create it.

---

## III. Script or Speaking Notes (10 pts)

(Marked with **[Story/Example]**, **[Evidence/Citation]**, **[Persuasive Phrase]**, **[Call to Action]**)

Good morning classmates and Professor Maija! Close your eyes for a second and imagine that: at a dinner table, lights warm, your parents lean in with sincere voice: “Honey, pick a stable job you like—doctor, lawyer, or civil servant. They'll make you decent, secure and respectful.”​ **[Hook Scene]**

Now open your eyes. Felt that stomach knot? Questions start hovering over our head: “What if we hate hospitals or courts?” “What if those so-called ‘stable’ feels empty?” “Is ‘decent’ only what seniors say?”​

We’ve all been there—trapped between the love of our parents and the quiet voice of our own desires. We’ll spend 90,000 hours at work—nearly one third of our lives. So these aren’t over-thoughts; they’re about whether we’ll live our own life, not just repeating someone else’s. **[Persuasive Phrase] Today, let’s turn the doubt to clarity: Young people must courageously challenge traditional career expectations.​**

---

For decades, traditional career expectations have been like invisible rules guiding us—prioritize stability over passion, prefer others' approval rather than individual happiness. But in today’s fast-evolving job market, those are quickly becoming outdated. **Blindly following them brings two big risks:​**

**Career burnout comes first.** **[Story/Example]** My uncle was a bank teller for 10 years, which his parents hailed as “iron rice bowl”. His daily tasks are just forced smiles and repetitive tasks, he always dreaming of photography but delaying it over and over again. Now 40, he quit the job to make a change with no experience, low-paid and amateur gears. “I wasted years on unhappiness,” he said.​

**Then arrives missing emerging opportunities.** A decade ago, AI trainers, sustainability consultants or metaverse designers didn’t even exist— but now they’re in high demand. Meanwhile, traditional jobs shrink: **[Evidence/Citation]** bank tellers dropped 35% in 5 years in China, replaced by tech. Stuck in old views, we lose decision-making skills too—**[Story/Example]** like my classmate Alex who was forced into finance by parents, now lost: “I never learned to choose what I want.”​

**[Evidence/Citation]** Data backs this: China Youth Daily’s 2024 report shows 58% of youth who followed tradition felt “dissatisfied” with jobs in 3 years. So, the old scripts fails—we need to explore new ones.​

---

Challenging tradition isn’t reckless—it’s intentional. Here are 3 simple steps:​

**First, follow your absorption: Not parents’ “you’re good at this,” but what makes you lose track of time.** **[Story/Example]** Just like the founder of Unitree Robotics, Wangxingxing who dives into the fields of robot from a young age: Happy and good jobs can be the same.

**Second, Research to build a plan: “Loved jobs don’t pay” is a myth.** **[Evidence/Citation]** It is reported that digital artists in the game _"Black Myth: Wukong"_ earns nearly 20 thousands per month, which is 20% higher than traditional entry-level jobs. So pick one interest like short-video production? Take a 3-month editing course, make 10 sample videos.​

**Third, talk to parents as partners: They oppose not to be stubborn, but to worry.** **[Story/Example]** My parents used to push me to computer science. But as I manifest my love for engineering and explain my plan for my future, they even actively support me to create some small works.

---

Imagine this future: On Monday mornings, you wake excited with no awful work. You’re making short videos you care about, building AI for the environment. Tired but proud, you can tell parents honestly: “I’m fulfilling.”​

This picture will be not just personal: we bring new passion to new fields. **[Evidence/Citation]** According to the World Economic Forum, 65% of 2030’s high-demand jobs don’t exist yet—this future belongs to us, the rule-breakers as well as the rule-makers.​

---

So, let’s call back the dinner table: Remember the doubt? Today, here’s our answer—firm and specific: We don’t settle for the past’s “only-decent choices.” We challenge old stereotypes and seek our own fortune.​

**[Call to Action]** Just start small this week:​ Spend 30 minutes writing what you love,​ research one career tied to it, and talk to parents with sincerity.

We don’t need all the answers at once—just start! Those 90,000 working hours do matter to us. Let’s stop imagining others’ lives, start building ours. **[Persuasive Phrase] We do not follow the future—we create it.​**

Thank you.​
